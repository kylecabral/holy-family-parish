FORMED

Our Parish Access Code is: GDC829 (case sensitive)

We are excited to announce that our parish has become a partner in a dynamic faith formation program called FORMED. This program is designed to help our parish form adults in the Catholic faith, and can be used both here at the parish and in our own homes.

Getting Started

Log-in by clicking on the "REGISTER" box and then enter the parish access code (GDC829) and your email address.

How Can I Use It?

As a member of our parish, you can access FORMED anytime on the web, using your computer, smartphone, or tablet, both here and at home. It is extremely important that we as Catholics not only know our Catholic faith, but also bear witness to Christ in our daily lives, particularly in this time of the New Evangelization. We strongly encourage every parishioner to take advantage of this excellent resource and make it a priority to learn more about our faith so that we can not only live it out more deeply, but also share it with others.

Some Ways to Use It Effectively

  • As a married couple: pick a program and work through it together
  • As a family: parents are the primary catechists of their children; use FORMED to teach and learn with your children
  • As a small group: the Church has always been about "small groups"; invite friends, family, and/or neighbors to be a part of a small group that uses FORMED to guide you in learning, praying, and discussing the Catholic faith

Accessing Materials

Visit formed.org, and our Parish Access Code is: GDC829 (case sensitive). From there, you can make your own log-in and get started today.

Programs Included

Symbolon

Symbolon is an immersion in the faith for the whole parish. It aims at transforming adults on all levels—how we think, how we pray, and how we live—by immersing us in instruction about the Word of God and the Catholic Faith. The 20 episodes in this program will take you through the entire story of the Catholic faith including topics such as Sacred Scripture, Salvation History, Jesus, the Church, Mary & the Saints, the Sacraments, Catholic Morality, God's Plan for Human Sexuality, and Catholic Social Teaching.

Beloved

A marriage preparation and marriage enrichment program from Augustine Institute. In this program, you encounter the story of your love for each other in the bigger story of God's love as revealed in the Bible, the flow of history, and Church teaching. Topics include: God's Plan for Marriage, The Truth About Love, Physical, Emotional and Spiritual Intimacy, Living Out the Sacrament of Marriage, Conflict, Healing, and Forgiveness, and Protecting the Bonds of Your Marriage.

Lectio

A program using video presentations and reading, reflection, response, and contemplation. There are two scriptural study programs available within Lectio—one from Dr. Tim Gray focusing on Peter the Apostle, and another from Dr. Brant Pitre focusing on the Eucharist.

YDisciple

A program of youth ministry. Although the parish uses the Life Teen program, the resources from YDisciple may be used to help parents learn how to foster discipleship in their teens in conjunction with their participation in youth ministry.

Movies, Audio & E-Books

Inspirational Catholic movies are available through our parish subscription to FORMED! You can also listen to a selection of audio recordings provided by Lighthouse Catholic Media, and access e-books and book excerpts.

RCIA - Becoming Catholic

Rite of Christian Initiation for Adults

About RCIA

The Rite of Christian Initiation for Adults (RCIA) is a process through which adults become members of the Catholic Church. It is a journey of faith that includes prayer, study, and reflection on the teachings of the Catholic Church.

Who Is RCIA For?

  • Those who have never been baptized
  • Those baptized in another Christian tradition who wish to become Catholic
  • Baptized Catholics who have not received First Communion or Confirmation

The Process

RCIA typically begins in the fall and culminates at the Easter Vigil, when those who have been preparing receive the sacraments of initiation. The process includes:

  • Weekly classes and discussions
  • Prayer and reflection
  • Participation in parish life
  • Rites and rituals throughout the year

Getting Started

If you are interested in learning more about the Catholic faith or are ready to begin the journey, contact the parish office. There is no obligation to commit—the first phase is a time of inquiry and asking questions.
