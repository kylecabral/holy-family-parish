Respect Life Ministry

About the Respect Life Ministry

The Respect Life Ministry focuses on the four major pillars that the United States Conference of Catholic Bishops have established.

The following is a list of the four pillars, and some sample ways that our ministry might incorporate the pillars in our work:

Public Information and Education

Planning activities, organizing speakers, movie nights with debriefs, etc.

Pastoral Care

Promoting Project Rachel, Sidewalk ministries, supporting the elderly, disability awareness, etc.

Public Policy

Helping organize letter-writing campaigns, postcard campaigns, and legislative initiatives, etc.

Prayer and Worship

Planning Masses for Life, Holy Hours, Prayer Services, etc.

This ministry advocates for ALL life from conception to natural death. All are welcome to attend our meetings, events, and activities!

"In our present social context, marked by a dramatic struggle between the 'culture of life' and the 'culture of death', there is need to develop a deep critical sense, capable of discerning true values and authentic needs. What is urgently called for is a general mobilization of consciences and a united ethical effort to activate a great campaign in support of life. All together, we must build a new culture of life." — Blessed John Paul II, The Gospel of Life, no. 95

St. Vincent dePaul Society

The Society of St. Vincent dePaul is a lay Catholic organization inspired by Gospel values to grow spiritually by offering person-to-person service to those who are in need or suffering.

Our Mission

Our members, known as Vincentians, are drawn from every ethnic and cultural background, age group, and economic level. We are united in an international society of charity grows by person-to-person involvement.

Services We Provide

  • Emergency food assistance
  • Help with utility bills and rent
  • Clothing and household items
  • Referrals to community resources
  • Home visits for those unable to travel

Get Help or Volunteer

Contact the parish office to request assistance or learn about volunteer opportunities. Donations of non-perishable food and cleaning supplies are always appreciated.

Music Ministry

Adult Choir

The adult choir provides music for the major parish liturgical celebrations. In addition, they sing at the Vigil Mass throughout the year. The choir rehearses on Thursday evenings from 6:30 p.m. to 8:30 p.m.

It is not necessary for choir members to know how to read music. The only requirement is the love of service and a desire to grow deeper in sung prayer. If interested in participating in the choir, let us know. All are welcome!

Children's Choir

The children's choir meets from Labor Day through Memorial Day and typically sings at the 9:00 Mass on the fourth Sunday of each month. Rehearsals are typically held on the first three Sundays of the month from 11:15 - 12:15 in the parish center.

If your child is interested in participating, he or she should be able to read and participate in the practices. For more information, please contact Mary Katherine at MKFletcher92@outlook.com. All are welcome!

Prayer Shawl Ministry

Our Prayer Shawl Ministry creates handmade shawls, lap robes, and other items that are blessed and given to those who are ill, grieving, or in need of comfort. Each item is crafted with prayer and love.

What We Make

  • Prayer shawls for those who are ill
  • Lap robes for nursing home residents
  • Baby blankets for baptism families
  • Chemo caps for cancer patients
  • Items for homeless shelters

Meetings

Ministry members are present the second Wednesday of each month from 1:00-2:30pm at the parish center. Attendance at the meeting is not required. Information regarding the ministry is available at the entrances to the church and at the parish center in both English and Spanish.

Join Us

All skill levels welcome - we can teach you! Yarn and patterns provided. Come knit, crochet, and pray with us.

Liturgical Ministries

Our liturgical ministries help enhance the worship experience at Holy Family Parish. These dedicated volunteers serve in various roles to assist with the celebration of Mass and other liturgical events.

Opportunities to Serve

  • Extraordinary Ministers of Holy Communion - Distribute the Eucharist at Mass
  • Lectors - Proclaim the Scripture readings at Mass
  • Altar Servers - Assist the priest during Mass
  • Ushers - Welcome parishioners and take up the collection
  • Sacristans - Prepare the altar and sanctuary for Mass
  • Greeters - Welcome visitors and parishioners before Mass

Training Provided

Training is provided for all liturgical roles. If you feel called to serve in any of these ministries, please contact the parish office to learn more about getting involved.

Knights of Columbus #11510

Holy Family Council #11510 is an organization of Catholic men founded on the principles of charity, unity, and fraternity.

Goals

  • To strengthen ourselves and each other in the Catholic faith
  • To strengthen our families and our family life
  • To be a strong pillar of support for our priests
  • To be of service to our Church and community by coming to the aid of those in need

Meetings

Council #11510 meets at 7 p.m. on the 1st Wednesday of every month at Holy Family Parish Center, 438 Middleboro Ave, East Taunton MA 02718.
