Sacraments

Encountering Christ through the sacraments of the Church

The seven sacraments are the foundation of the Catholic Church's worship and the primary means by which God's grace is mediated to us. They are visible signs of an invisible reality - outward signs that give grace to those who receive them with faith.

At Holy Family Parish, we are privileged to accompany you on your sacramental journey. Whether you are preparing for Baptism, First Communion, Confirmation, Matrimony, or any of the other sacraments, our parish community is here to support you.

☨

Baptism

The first sacrament of initiation and the door to the other sacraments

About the Sacrament

Baptism is the first of the three Sacraments of Initiation. Through Baptism, we are freed from original sin, become children of God, and are incorporated into the Church. It is the foundation of the Christian life and the gateway to life in the Spirit.

At Holy Family Parish

Baptisms are celebrated on the second and fourth Sundays of each month. Parents are required to attend a preparation class before their child's baptism. This class helps parents understand the significance of the sacrament and their role in raising their child in the Catholic faith.

Requirements

  • Parents must be registered parishioners or have permission from their home parish
  • Parents must attend a baptism preparation class
  • Godparents must be practicing Catholics, at least 16 years old, and have received all sacraments of initiation
  • At least one godparent must be a confirmed Catholic

How to Schedule

Contact the parish office to schedule your child's baptism and to register for the preparation class. We recommend contacting us at least two months before your desired date.

First Communion

Receiving the Body and Blood of Christ for the first time

About the Sacrament

The Eucharist is the source and summit of the Christian life. In the Eucharist, we receive the Body and Blood of Jesus Christ, who is truly present under the appearance of bread and wine. First Communion marks the first time a person receives this holy sacrament.

Preparation at Holy Family

Children prepare for First Communion through our Religious Education program. The preparation is typically a two-year process beginning in first grade. During this time, children learn about the Mass, the meaning of the Eucharist, and how to receive Communion reverently.

Requirements

  • Child must be baptized (baptismal certificate required)
  • Two years of religious education preparation
  • Regular attendance at Religious Education classes
  • Attendance at Sunday Mass each week

First Communion Celebration

First Communion is typically celebrated in the spring of second grade. Families are invited to celebrate this special day with their children, and the parish community joins in welcoming these new communicants to the Eucharistic table.

Confirmation

Completing Christian initiation and being sealed with the Holy Spirit

About the Sacrament

Confirmation completes the sacraments of initiation, following Baptism and First Communion. Through Confirmation, we receive the full outpouring of the Holy Spirit as the apostles did on Pentecost. The sacrament strengthens our bond with the Church and helps us bear witness to the Christian faith.

For Youth

Young people prepare for Confirmation through our Religious Education program. Preparation typically begins in eighth grade or high school and includes classes, service projects, and spiritual formation. The Confirmation Mass is celebrated with the Bishop or his delegate.

For Adults

Adults who are baptized but have not yet been confirmed should contact the parish office. Preparation sessions are offered periodically, and adults are often confirmed at the Easter Vigil or at a special Mass with the Bishop.

Requirements

  • Must be baptized (baptismal certificate required)
  • Must have received First Communion
  • Completion of preparation program
  • Selection of a Confirmation saint name and sponsor

Matrimony

The sacrament of marriage, a covenant of love and life

About the Sacrament

Matrimony is a sacrament in which a baptized man and woman enter into a covenant with each other and with God. This sacred union is a sign of Christ's love for the Church. The couple themselves are the ministers of this sacrament, witnessed by the Church.

Marriage Preparation

The Diocese of Fall River requires a minimum six-month preparation period before the wedding. This preparation helps couples develop a strong foundation for their married life together.

Preparation Requirements

  • Contact the parish office at least six months before your desired date
  • Meet with the priest or deacon for initial interview
  • Complete Pre-Cana or similar marriage preparation program
  • Complete FOCCUS or similar inventory for engaged couples
  • Obtain necessary documents (baptismal certificates, etc.)
  • Plan the liturgy with the officiating priest or deacon

Weddings at Holy Family

We are honored to celebrate your wedding at Holy Family Parish. Please contact the parish office to check available dates and to begin the preparation process.

Anointing of the Sick

Healing and strength for those facing serious illness or surgery

About the Sacrament

Anointing of the Sick is a sacrament of healing. It is for those who are seriously ill, facing major surgery, or whose health is seriously impaired by old age. Through this sacrament, the Church commends those who are ill to the suffering and glorified Lord, asking that He raise them up and save them.

When to Request

You should request the Anointing of the Sick when:

  • You are facing a serious illness
  • You are preparing for major surgery
  • Your health is seriously impaired by old age
  • You are facing a serious medical crisis

How to Request

Contact the parish office to request the Anointing of the Sick. For hospital visits, please let us know before admission if possible. In case of emergency, call the parish office at any time.

For the Homebound

For those who are homebound and wish to receive the Anointing of the Sick, Holy Communion, or a pastoral visit, please contact the parish office to arrange a visit.

Godparents & Sponsors

Asking Someone to Be a Godparent or Sponsor?

These roles are significant in the Church and have special requirements. Please contact Fr. Matt Gill (frgill@holyfamilytaunton.org) or Deacon Kevin (kpgingras@gmail.com) to discuss the qualifications and to acquire the necessary paperwork.

Have You Been Asked to Be a Godparent or Sponsor?

Are you ready to take on this responsibility? Reflect on the following questions:

  • Do you have a strong prayer life?
  • Do you regularly receive God's grace through the Sacrament of Reconciliation?
  • Do you worship God weekly at Mass?
  • Do you study the Catholic faith?
  • Do you give of your time, talent, and treasure to the Church?
  • Do you consider yourself ready to share the Good News of Jesus Christ with others?
  • Did you receive the Sacrament of Confirmation?
  • If you are married, were you married in a Catholic Church by a priest?

If you answered "yes" to all of these questions then you are ready to take on this important role as a disciple. If you answered "no" to any of these questions, but have a desire to become or grow as a disciple then please contact us.
